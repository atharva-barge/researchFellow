Unveiling the Potential: Biodegradation of Plastics by Algae : A Review

This review explores the potential of microalgae in plastic biodegradation. Species like Chlamydomonas reinhardtii and Chlorella vulgaris use enzymes to break down plastics, offering a sustainable solution to "white pollution." It summarizes research from the past seven years and highlights future prospects in eco-friendly waste management.
